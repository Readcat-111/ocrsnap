# OCR Snap — Privacy Policy **Last updated: July 8, 2026** OCR Snap is built to respect your privacy. The short version: **your images and extracted text never leave your PC, and we don't collect anything about you.** ## What we collect **Nothing.** OCR Snap has no accounts, no sign-in, and no analytics or telemetry. We do not collect, transmit, store, or sell any personal information. ## How your data is handled - **Screen captures & images** you scan are processed **entirely on your device** using on-device OCR (Windows' built-in OCR and the bundled Tesseract engine). They are never uploaded to us or anyone else. - **Extracted text** is placed on your Windows clipboard (so you can paste it) and, if you choose, saved to your **local scan history**. This history lives only in your app-data folder on your PC and is never transmitted. - **Clipboard access** is used only when you ask OCR Snap to scan a copied image, and only to read that image and to write the recognized text back for you. - **Exports** (TXT/PDF) are saved to the location you choose on your PC. ## Network use OCR Snap works fully offline. The **only** time it makes a network request is when **you explicitly choose to download an additional language pack**, which is fetched from the public Tesseract language-data repository on GitHub. That request contains no personal information — only the name of the language file being downloaded. ## Children's privacy OCR Snap does not collect any data from anyone, including children. ## Changes to this policy If a future version adds an optional cloud-powered feature (for example, AI recognition), it will be clearly labelled, strictly opt-in, and covered by an updated version of this policy before it processes anything off your device. ## Contact Questions about this policy?
